Overview

Ununennium (Uue) is the provisional IUPAC name for element 119, the first undiscovered element in Period 8. It would be the heaviest alkali metal ever synthesized, positioned directly below Francium in Group 1.

With a single valence electron in the 8s¹ subshell, it follows the same chemical pattern as Lithium, Sodium, and Potassium. However, at this atomic mass, relativistic effects become dominant — the inner electrons travel at a significant fraction of the speed of light, contracting the s-orbitals and altering expected chemical behaviour.

Unlike lighter alkali metals, Uue's valence electron would be more tightly bound than simple trends predict, making it less reactive than Francium — a direct consequence of relativistic orbital contraction.

Researchers at RIKEN (Japan) have attempted to produce element 119 by colliding Berkelium-249 nuclei with Vanadium-51 ions, but no confirmed detection has been reported as of 2026. The search continues at GSI (Germany) and other heavy-ion facilities worldwide.
